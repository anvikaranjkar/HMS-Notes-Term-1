Q9 — approaches to health analyse · 8 marks
key term
ANALYSE = implication sentence each para
marks + time
8 marks · 16 minutes
acronym
BASES: Biomedical / Behavioural / Socioenvironmental
para 1 — biomedical
  • Health = absence of disease; diagnosis, treatment, cure
  • Influenced: vaccination programs (polio/measles eliminated); cancer screening (BreastScreen, bowel); Medicare
  • Limitation: reactive — treats after development; ignores social determinants; expensive
"An implication of this is that relying on biomedical alone produces a system unable to prevent the chronic diseases now leading causes of death in Australia."
para 2 — behavioural / lifestyle
  • Individual responsible through personal choices (diet, exercise, smoking, alcohol)
  • Influenced: Quit (smoking 30%→12%); SunSmart; Physical Activity Guidelines; Healthy Harold
  • Limitation: "victim blaming" — ignores structural barriers; works best for those who already have resources
"An implication of this is that behavioural campaigns without structural change risk widening rather than closing health inequalities."
para 3 — socioenvironmental / Ottawa Charter (1986)
  • Health shaped by social, economic, physical environments — not just biology/behaviour
  • 5 action areas: healthy public policy · supportive environments · community action · personal skills · reorient health services
  • Influenced: smoke-free laws; health-promoting schools; community health centres; National Mental Health Strategy
  • Most comprehensive — addresses root determinants not just symptoms
"An implication of this is that the socioenvironmental approach is the most transformative, as it is the only one that directly attacks the conditions that produce illness in the first place."
Structure: short intro → 3 body paras. Each: define → Australian examples → limitation → implication. No implication = can't reach 7–8 band.
